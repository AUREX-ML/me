About

Ayiemba Rodgers goes by Aurex — a professional identity that reflects his approach: precise, systematic, and built to operate at scale.

He is a systems thinker who works across engineering, software development, and business. His focus is the African energy sector, where fragmented grids, limited monitoring infrastructure, and rapidly growing distributed energy resources create both a hard technical problem and a genuine commercial opportunity.

His primary venture, enerOS, is an energy operating system for distributed energy resource management — built on OpenEMS and designed to give utilities, aggregators, and energy developers real-time visibility and control over solar, storage, EV charging, and flexible loads.

The long-term ambition, neOS, is to use enerOS as the foundation for modernising national utility infrastructure across Africa — starting in Kenya, scaling county by county, then nationally, then across the continent.

Aurex is motivated by Mission 300 — the goal of expanding electricity access to 300 million people across Africa.

enerOS

Energy operating system for distributed energy resource management.

enerOS is a software platform that enables utilities, aggregators, and energy developers to monitor, control, and optimise distributed energy resources (DERs) — including solar, storage, EV charging, and flexible loads.

Built on open standards (OpenEMS, IEC 61968/61970, OCPP) and designed to operate at the grid edge.

What it provides

  • Real-time DER monitoring and control
  • Virtual power plant (VPP) orchestration
  • Grid services — frequency response, demand flexibility
  • Energy market participation interfaces
  • Developer API for third-party integrations

The problem

African energy grids are fragmented, under-monitored, and increasingly dependent on distributed generation that existing utility systems cannot manage.

Current milestone

Five-site commercial and industrial deployment in Kenya — proving the system works before scaling.

Long-term scope

Facility → County → National → East Africa → Africa
